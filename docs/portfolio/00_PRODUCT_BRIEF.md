# 00 — Product Brief

## Portfolio as a product

This is not a personal website. It is a product with a defined user, a defined goal, and a defined success condition. Every design decision must serve that.

---

## Primary user

**Munich-based HR recruiter or hiring manager** at a mid-to-large tech company, SaaS business, or product-led organisation. They have 30–90 seconds per portfolio at first pass. They are experienced with design portfolios and can immediately tell when work is generic, inflated, or AI-generated in bulk.

**What they need to know, in order:**
1. What kind of designer this is
2. What product types they have worked on
3. Whether the work demonstrates real complexity and product thinking
4. Whether they are based in Munich and eligible to work in Germany
5. Whether the work is credible — no invented metrics, no fake launches

**What kills credibility immediately:**
- "Impact: 40% increase in conversions" with no client or context
- Figma screens without any product thinking behind them
- AI workflow presented as a capability rather than a tool
- Generic design system slides that could be from any junior designer

---

## Product positioning

**Boris Milosavac** is a senior product designer with 14+ years of hands-on experience. His strongest work is at the intersection of product complexity and operational clarity: B2B dashboards, multi-role platforms, e-commerce browsing, and reusable UI systems.

**Differentiators:**
- Senior-level product thinking, not just visual execution
- AI-assisted exploration with human-led review and judgment
- Accessibility-aware product decisions embedded in the work, not added at the end
- Honesty about project status: concept, MVP, real e-commerce — clearly labelled
- Munich-based, authorised to work in Germany, English fluent, learning German

**Positioning sentence:**
> I design complex digital products that people can actually use.

This holds. Do not change it. It is direct, credible, and scoped.

## Portfolio goals

| Goal | Metric (qualitative) |
|---|---|

---

## Content rules

1. **No fake metrics.** If a number cannot be sourced, it does not appear.
2. **No fake launches.** If the product was not shipped to real users, it is labelled concept, MVP, or prototype.
3. **No invented quotes.** No testimonials without real attribution.
4. **No visible placeholders.** Every section ships complete or does not ship.
5. **AI is a tool, not a claim.** One clear section explains the workflow. No "AI-powered portfolio" language anywhere.
6. **Truth layer per case.** Each case ends with a short honest statement of what is claimed and what is not.

---

## Tone

- Direct. No adjective inflation.
- Senior. Confident without overselling.
- Honest. Actively limits claims to what is true.
- Calm. No urgency copy. No "I'm available now" desperation signals.

---

## Constraints

- Do not change the technology stack.
- Do not add dependencies.
- Do not rebuild from scratch.
- Do not introduce placeholder content that ships to production.
- Redesign is cosmetic and structural — not a content replacement.
